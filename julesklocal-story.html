Read the story
Chapter 01

The problem wasn't a lack of services.
It was a lack of access.

The services exist. The doctors are out there. The local businesses are open. The home cooks are cooking. The musicians are making music.

But finding them? That's where everything falls apart.

Hospital portals that don't answer questions. Insurance searches that return nothing useful. Google results full of paid placements. Yelp charging small businesses a fortune just to be found. Meta running the only "community" anyone can afford.

The system isn't broken. It's designed — just not for us.

✦
Chapter 02

I needed to find a doctor.
It was the worst.

Not because doctors don't exist. Because navigating insurance portals to find one that's local, available, and actually covered felt like a second job.

Copy-pasting resource lists. Calling numbers that go to voicemail. Clicking through portals built in 2008 that aren't question-friendly.

"The good services are hard to find. The searches are the worst. There's no worse than the worst."

That frustration didn't go away. It turned into a question: what if this was just... easy?

✦
Chapter 03

What if your neighbor
could feed you tonight?

Community isn't an app feature. It's people. A home cook making an extra plate. A musician whose song you heard once and can't stop thinking about. A local business that actually picks up the phone.

These things already exist. They just don't have a place to be found — without someone taking 30% off the top, without fake reviews, without a paywall just to be visible.

JuleskLocal is that place.
